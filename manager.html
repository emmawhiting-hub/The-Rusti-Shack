About this model

How reorder points & stock levels are set

What a reorder point is

The reorder point is the stock level at which you should place a new order. Set it right and a fresh shipment arrives just as you're about to run out — you avoid empty shelves without overstocking.

The formula

Reorder point = lead-time demand + safety stock. Lead-time demand is how much you expect to sell while you wait for the restock to arrive; safety stock is a cushion for weeks that sell more than usual.

Lead-time demand

We assume it takes about two weeks to restock, so lead-time demand is roughly half of the item's forecast for the coming month.

Safety stock

Sized to how much the item's monthly sales bounce around: 1.28 × its month-to-month sales swing (the standard deviation of recent monthly units). Steady sellers get a small cushion; erratic ones get a bigger one. The 1.28 targets about a 90% chance of not running out while you wait on a restock.

Suggested stock level

The "Suggested" figure is roughly one month of forecast demand plus the safety stock — enough to comfortably cover a normal month between orders.

Where the demand forecast comes from

Each item's expected monthly sales are its recent average, adjusted by its own seasonal pattern — so a sunscreen's summer bump or a wetsuit's winter dip is baked in.

What the flags mean

Stockout — nothing on the shelf, reorder immediately. Reorder — at or below the reorder point, time to restock. OK — comfortably above it.
